The Philippines is facing a series of typhoons, including Typhoon Nika (Toraji), which made landfall in Aurora province on November 11. Thousands of families were evacuated, and extensive damage to homes and infrastructure was reported. Another storm, Typhoon Ofel, is expected to follow, further complicating recovery efforts. These events follow a devastating month of storms that have displaced over 700,000 people

Heavy rains in the Valencia region of Spain triggered severe flooding. As of November 17, emergency services are addressing widespread damage to buildings, roads, and agricultural areas. Thousands have been displaced, and efforts to map and assess the impact are ongoing

Mount Lewotobi Laki-Laki in Flores, Indonesia, erupted twice this month, causing nine fatalities and leaving 64 injured. Over 10,000 residents have been evacuated due to the continuing volcanic activity. Homes and a school were destroyed by falling debris and volcanic ash

Persistent rainfall has caused significant flooding in Nigeria and Honduras, displacing thousands. In Honduras, Tropical Storm Sara brought torrential rains, overwhelming infrastructure and forcing emergency evacuations
